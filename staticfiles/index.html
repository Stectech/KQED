YOU ARE ESSENTIAL.

KQED has been a media leader more than 60 years. Our vision continues: to be the most reliable source for original Bay Area content for you, your children and your grandchildren, providing the information, lifelong learning, and public discourse that are essential to the civic and cultural health of our community. We see an enormous opportunity to serve you better.

YOU ARE KQED.

You are INFORMED

KQED has long been a valued source for NPR and PBS programming, but now our role as the Bay Area’s principal source of local content that is free, high quality, and intelligent is more important than ever. The digital revolution has reduced local media outlets drastically. We must take a profound new look at KQED’s responsibility to inform, educate, engage and inspire our community.

You are INSPIRED

KQED ignites imaginations with scientific discovery, technological innovation and artistic creation. Moving into the future, KQED will be the Bay Area’s curator—a trusted guide to cut through the noise and deliver what inspires, educates and moves you. If it’s on KQED, you’ll know it’s intelligent, informative and important and that you are learning something that will enrich your life.

You are CURIOUS

Education has to reach beyond textbooks. In the wake of shrinking school budgets and growing achievement gaps, multimedia classrooms need to cultivate curiosity and critical thinking skills in students, creating thought leaders, global citizens and lifelong learners. With your support, KQED will deliver effective learning tools, information and training to teachers, students and curious learners everywhere.

You are ENGAGED

KQED’s free and vibrant exchange of ideas strengthens our community, but we must adapt to a changing audience that expects their media on-demand. Our new KQED Lab will develop cutting edge content and delivery mechanisms, inventive sharing tools, and new funding models to ensure KQED continues to reach and engage our audience where they are, when they wish, and on the platform they prefer.
